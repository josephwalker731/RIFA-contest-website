  <h4>who we are</h4>
  <p>RIFA is a local, faith based organization that has been a part of the Jackson community since 1976.</p>
  <br>
  <h4>our vision</h4>
  <p>To lead and engage the community in the fight against hunger.</p>
  <br>
  <h4>our mission</h4>
  <p>To reach out with the love of Christ to provide physical and spiritual nourishment.</p>
  <br>
  <h4>safe place</h4>
  <p>As much as we are all about fighting hunger, we are a safe place for people to find community and experience Christ.</p>
  <br>
  <h4>why we exist</h4>
<p>Because 1 out of 5 people living in Jackson are unsure of where their next meal is coming from. 18.9% of people in Madison County live below the poverty level which is higher than the 15% US average.</p>

  <p>
    <img src="https://codehs.com/uploads/0e3cdb1ea631d017c821c32f37832132" class="pic">
    <br>
    This Super Bowl Sunday, February 7th, RIFA will be joining in the Souper Bowl of Caring, a national movement of people working together around the time of the game to fight hunger and poverty in their local communities. This idea came from a simple prayer, “Lord, even as we enjoy the Super Bowl football game, help us be mindful of those that do not have a bowl of soup to eat.”

  </p>

  <h1>OUR HISTORY TIMELINE</h1>
  <hr>
  <p><b>1976</b> – RIFA was established with Rev. Gary Scheidt as the first Executive Director. The office was located in Jones Hall at the old Union University in downtown Jackson.</p>
  <hr>
<p><b>1977-1978</b> – RIFA coordinated Food Fairs in the parking lot of the old West Jackson Baptist Church, which was the beginning of today’s Farmers Market.</p>
<hr>
<p><b>1992</b> – RIFA moved to the old Clean Linen building at 219 N. Highland Avenue.</p>
<hr>
<p><b>1996</b> – The RIFA Thrift Store opens.</p>
<hr>
<p><b>1996</b> –The local Community Soup Kitchen became one of RIFA’s ministries.</p>
<hr>
<p><b>2005</b> – Snack Backpack ministry started with one school and 25 children served.</p>
<hr>
<p><b>2005</b> – RIFA begins operating an independent Food Bank.</p>
<hr>
<p><b>2005</b> – First annual Canstruction fundraiser held at the Jackson Fairgrounds.</p>
<hr>
<p><b>2009</b> – Lisa Tillman becomes Executive Director of RIFA.</p>
<hr>
<p><b>2012</b> – Moved to the former Nando Jones building at 133 Airways Boulevard.</p>
<hr>
<p><b>2014</b> – Canstruction moved to the Civic Center on its 10th anniversary.</p>
<hr>
<p><b>2014</b> – RIFA was named the Jackson Chamber’s Nonprofit of the Year.</p>
<hr>
<p><b>2015</b> – RIFA graduates from the Alliance for Nonprofit Excellence’s Program for Nonprofit Excellence (PNE).</p>
<hr>
<p><b>2019</b> – RIFA started the Bus Stop Cafe.</p>
<hr>
<p><b>2019</b> – RIFA distrubuted 518,136 meals.</p>
